Call to Action

The Graph

The graph depicts the rise of social media and the increase in the rate of major depressive episodes. The red line shows the percentage of social media usage throughout the years. The bar graphs show the increase of major depressive episodes through the years 2005-2017. The green bar graph represents the increase for the age group of 12-17. The blue bar graph represents the increase for the age group of 18-25.

The Problem

Social media plays a role in the increase of depression throughout the years. The rise of social media can be one cause for a rise in the percent of depressed adolescents. If technology continues to develop, social media will continue to expand and reach more people. Furthermore mental health needs to be normalized in society today so that we can recognize the toxic traits like social media that are causing an increase in mental illness. *Social media is not the sole cause of mental illnesses because there are many reasons one has a mental illness.

Possible Solutions

Limit social media useage! While it is hard to eliminate social media from society today, as individuals we can limit how much we are on our phone. Also it is important to realize that social media is a powerful tool that only portrays a part of someones life. Additionally, be kind to others because you do not know what is fully happening in their life.
